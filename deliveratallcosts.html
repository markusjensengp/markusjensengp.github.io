Deliver At All Costs

Project Info

  • Length: ~2 months
  • Engine: Unity
  • Platform: PC, PS5, Xbox Series S & X

Summary

Deliver At All Costs is a thrilling action game where destruction, absurdity and intrigue collide! I worked as a programming intern at Far Out Games, fixing various features and bugs.

Watch the trailer here.

My Contribution

I joined Far Out Games as an intern during the last few months of development on this title. The main focus at this stage was on polishing the game, which involved fixing bugs and improving existing gameplay mechanics. Even though I joined late in the project, I still had the chance to contribute with my ideas and improvements to the project. I got to work on most of the game's core systems, both completing assigned tasks and suggesting improvements.

Bug Fixing

My assigned tasks were diverse and allowed me to work on most of the game's systems, learning about the codebase and overall structure while contributing to the project and learning the engine. From this, I gained valuable experience in both debugging and how to join a team effectively, as well as improving my problem-solving skills.

The bugs I worked on ranged from simple null reference exceptions, input handling issues, or sound effect issues, to more complex problems like mission-related issues.

Feature Improvements

A feature that I worked on was an obtainable upgrade to the player car, more specifically the jet engine upgrade. This upgrade allowed players to boost their speed and fly through the streets. It was designed to give the player a shorter boost of speed, but it was a slight issue with it.
After playtesting the upgrade, I found that it was quite easy to use the boost in order to propell yourself upwards, reaching places that were not intended to be reached. To fix this, I limit boost depending on the car's upwards angle, ensuring that players could still use it to gain speed without flying into unintended areas.

View Snippet 

float scalar = Vector3.Dot(carForward.XZ().normalized, carForward);
m_playerCarRigidBody.AddForce(carForward * (m_power * falloff * scalar), ForceMode.Acceleration);

Another issue with this upgrade was the speed, it was simply too fast. So I was tasked with adding a top speed without affecting the boost acceleration or duration. This was solved by adding a force that counteracted the boost force when the car reached a certain speed.
